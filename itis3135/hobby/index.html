Where was the inspiration for KRnB?

From the West to the East

In the start of the '40s, Korean music was mostly dominated from genres heavily inspired from their neighbors.

Thus, music genres such as trot, the Korean version of the Japanese enka, and pansori, Korean storytelling opera which was inspired from Chinese opera, was dominating the music scene.

However, after the rise of popularity of R&B and Pop, it slowly started to get introduced into the US as well as Koreans producing their own interpretations of the genres in the form of KRnB and KPop.

From the first exposure of the native version of these songs through Seo Taeji and The Boys in the '90s, KRnB and KPop have become an interest in Korea

The Rise of Boy and Girl Bands

After the success of Seo Taeji and the Boys, several artists started to emerge pursuing these music ventures.

Artists like Jin Young Park (JYP) and Yang Shin Hyuk (YG), an ex-member of Seo Taeji, started to form their own entertainment companies after the initial boom of the genre.

At this time period, a lot of boy groups and girl groups started to emerge where groups such as HOT, G.O.D, Sechskies, and S.E.S dominated the market.

Thus overtime they started to develop their own sounds where it incorporated a mixture of several genres such as Pop, Ballads, and RnB.

The Emergence of the Underground

After the success of these entertainment groups, several artists have started to emerge that didn't want to join the bigger entertainment companies.

Due to their intense training and their strict regimes, trainees who were not able to make it into these companies ended up starting their own ventures.

Thus, several other artist such as Tiger JK, Insooni, and YoonmiRae has emerged as well where a new age and genre of music emerged.

KHipHop, KRap and KR&B has started to develop further from these underground artists.

Modern Day

Nowadays there are a lot more artists that have been released and popularized in the underground that later became mainstream artists.

Such examples include Crush, DEAN, Hoody, and BIBI.

The genre now is striving as they release more and more artists, it can get overwhelming trying to figure out which artist is worth listening to.

Thus there is a page to help introduce visitors on some artists to listen to.
