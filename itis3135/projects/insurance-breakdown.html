Insurance Cost Breakdown

One of the most misunderstood things about insurance is the cost.

In this page, we will discuss about some key concepts so that you can save money!

Types of Payment

Fixed Costs (Always Paid)

  • Premium: The monthly or yearly fee you pay to keep your policy active. It's like a subscription.
  • Administrative Fees: Small charges for paperwork or late payments (may vary by provider).

Out-of-Pocket Costs (Paid When You Use the Insurance)

  • Deductible: The amount you pay before your insurance starts covering claims.
  • Copayment (Copay): A set fee you pay for specific services like a doctor's visit.
  • Coinsurance: A percentage of costs you pay after meeting your deductible (e.g., 20%).
  • Out-of-Pocket Maximum: The most you will have to pay in a year; insurance covers 100% after that.

Money Saving Tips

  • Low premium plans may have higher deductibles.
  • High premium plans may offer better coverage with less out-of-pocket risk.
  • Always read the plan's terms before signing up!
